Can't find golf ball


Can't find ball - What are my options?

If you are unable to find your golf ball, you must take stroke and distance relief. This means that you must return to the area where you hit your previous shot. You must take a drop and play your next shot from here. You also receive a one shot penalty, adding one shot to your overall score for the hole.


Example:

John hit his second shot into the trees and isn't able to locate his ball. He returns to the point where he hit the shot. Due to the one shot penalty, John is now taking his 4th shot from the point where he originally took his 2nd.

Local Rules


Some golf clubs may instate special rules for when a ball is lost but the location is certain. Check with your golf club before the round if this is the case.


How to drop a ball


Mark the point where you will be dropping (with a tee, pitch tool or other). A ball should be dropped from knee height straight down. If the ball rolls from the point that is marked, then you can drop it again. If it rolls more than twice then you can place the ball in the marked area by hand.




This information is organized in conjunction with rule 18 of the official rules of golf. For more information on this, see the official rules of golf website here.
