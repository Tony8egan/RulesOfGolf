White Stakes or White Markings

White stakes or White markings indicates out of bounds (OOB). If you lose your ball Out of Bounds you must go back to the point where you hit your shot out of bounds and hit another shot and will receive a one-shot penalty adding one shot to your final score for the hole.

Advised: Playing a provisional

If you think that your ball may have gone out of bounds after taking your shot but are not sure, it is advised that you play a provisional ball. A provisional ball is to be played as if you are taking your drop shot after seeing the ball may have gone out of bounds, however it will not count as the ball you are playing until you confirm that the original ball did go out of bounds. You can continue to play the provisional ball until you reach your original ball without it losing status as a ‘provisional’. This is intended to save time. You must announce to your playing partners that you are playing a provisional when you intend to do so.


When is my ball considered out of Bounds?


The line between two white stakes or a white line on the ground will determine where out of bounds begins. If all of the ball falls on the out of bounds line or beyond, it is considered out of bounds. If part of the ball falls on the line, it is considered in play.
