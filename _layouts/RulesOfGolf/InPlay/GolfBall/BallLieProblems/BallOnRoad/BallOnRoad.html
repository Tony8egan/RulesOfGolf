Ball on road

If a players golf ball lands on a road or path, this is considered to be an abnormal course condition where free relief is allowed. Use the playable area of the course closest to the ball as your reference point. If the abnormal course condition is also interfering with a players stance, they will be entitled to further relief. This means that if the reference point forces the player to stand on the path, the reference point can be moved further to the position that allows the player a stance on the course. The player can then take a free drop up to one club length from the reference point, no closer to the hole than the reference point.


See below how to take relief



How to drop a ball

Mark the point where you will be dropping (with a tee, pitch tool or other). A ball should be dropped from knee height straight down. If the ball rolls from the point that is marked, then you can drop it again. If it rolls more than twice then you can place the ball in the marked area by hand.
