Ball in divot

Unfortunately, you will not receive relief from divots and must play the ball as it lies.

If you don't feel that you can play the ball you can declare an unplayable. This will result in a one shot penalty. You can find more information on declaring unplayable here


What is a divot?

A divot is the mark made in the grass that is caused by a golfer when they take a stroke. The golfer will often brush the turf with their clubs leaving a mark on the course. Golfers are meant to re-cover their divots with the turf and grass(or sometimes with sand from sand/seed bags provided by the course) that they cut from the ground as this will speed up the recovery process for the grass.




This information is organized in conjunction with rule 16 of the official rules of golf. For more information on this, see the official rules of golf website here.
