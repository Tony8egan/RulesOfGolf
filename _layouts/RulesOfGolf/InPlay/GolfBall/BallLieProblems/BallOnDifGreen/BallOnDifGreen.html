Ball on different holes green

If your ball lands on a green belonging to a hole that you are not playing, you must take a free drop and are not to play the ball as it lies.

The reference point for taking a drop is the nearest point of relief i.e the closest point to the ball that is not the green. The player can then take a free drop up to one club length from the reference point, no closer to the hole than the reference point.


How to drop a ball

Mark the point where you will be dropping (with a tee, pitch tool or other). A ball should be dropped from knee height straight down. If the ball rolls from the point that is marked, then you can drop it again. If it rolls more than twice then you can place the ball in the marked area by hand.




This information is organized in conjunction with rule 13 of the official rules of golf. For more information on this, see the official rules of golf website here.
