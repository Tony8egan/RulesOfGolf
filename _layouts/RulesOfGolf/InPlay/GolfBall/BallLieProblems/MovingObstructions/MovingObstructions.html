Moving Obstructions


Removing loose impediment

Without penalty, a player may remove a loose impediment anywhere on or off the course, and may do so in any way (such as by using a hand or foot or a club or other equipment).

(1) A player must not deliberately remove a loose impediment that, if moved when the ball was at rest, would have been likely to have caused the ball to move.

(2) If the player does so, he or she gets one penalty stroke, but the removed loose impediment does not need to be replaced.

(1) Player was already allowed to Lift or Move Ball based on other rule

(2) Accidental Movement Before Ball Is Found

(3) Accidental Movement on Putting Green

(4) Accidental Movement Anywhere Except on Putting Green While Applying Rule

In all cases the ball should be returned to it's original position before moved.


Movable/Immovable Obstructions

An obstruction that can be moved with reasonable effort and without damaging the obstruction or the course.

If part of an immovable obstruction or integral object (such as a gate or door or part of an attached cable) meets these two standards, that part is treated as a movable obstruction.

But this does not apply if the movable part of an immovable obstruction or integral object is not meant to be moved (such as a loose stone that is part of a stone wall).

Even when an obstruction is movable, the Committee may define it to be an immovable obstruction.


Non Movable Obstructions


  • Tee Markers

    • Ball marker obstruction

    Oppponents ball marker blocks my line on the green?

    If a players ball marker appears to obstruct the path of your golf ball you can ask your opponent to move their marker.


    My ball marker blocks another players line on the green?

    If your ball marker is blocking the line of another players putt, you can move the marker parallel to it's position in accordance to the hole.

    Use your putter to temporarilly mark and move the ball marker. Place the tip your putter at the point where your ball marker is and use the opposite end as the new spot(while keeping the putter parallel to the hole position) for your ball marker. Make sure you move the marker back to it's original spot when you are replacing the ball to putt.
