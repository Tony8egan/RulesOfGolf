Ball in bunker


How to play out of bunkers

A ball that sits in a bunker is to be played similarly to other parts of the golf course, however

A player must not touch the sand or ground their club in the sand. The player must hover their club and only touch the sand when coming into contact with the golf ball during the down swing of the golf swing. If a player touches the sand, intentionally or unintentionally outside of taking their shot, they will incur a two shot penalty. In match play the player will automatically lose the hole for this infringement.


Loose impediment in bunker

A player is entitled to pick out loose impediment that is not sand but should do so in a way that makes minimal adjustment to the sand or ball position. This allows you to remove any stray rocks, leaves, or other debris that might find their way into the bunker.

I can't play the ball


If you feel that it isn't possible to play the ball as it lies in the bunker, you can declare an unplayable. This will allow you up to two club lengths of relief and result in a penalty. You can read about taking an unplayable here.
