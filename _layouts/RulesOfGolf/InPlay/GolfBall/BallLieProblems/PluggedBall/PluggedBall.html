Embedded / Plugged Ball

If your golf ball is considered to be embedded or plugged in the ground, you are entitled to a free drop of up to one club length from the reference point no closer to the hole. Use the spot right behind where the ball plugged as your reference point.


You are only entitled to relief in the general area of the golf course.

This excludes
  • All penalty areas
  • The teeing area the player must play from in starting the hole he or she is playing
  • All bunkers
  • The putting green of the hole the player is playing

    • How to drop a ball


    Mark the point where you will be dropping (with a tee, pitch tool or other). A ball should be dropped from knee height straight down. If the ball rolls from the point that is marked, then you can drop it again. If it rolls more than twice then you can place the ball in the marked area by hand.
