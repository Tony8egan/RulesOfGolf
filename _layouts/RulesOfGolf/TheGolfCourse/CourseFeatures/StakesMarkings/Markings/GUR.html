G.U.R - Ground Under Repair


What does this mean?


G.U.R signs stand for ground under repair. If an area is marked with this sign then you are entitled to free relief with no penalty. You can take a drop up to one club length from the reference point. The drop should be no closer to the hole, must be in the general area(excludes the green, teeing area, penalty area or bunkers) of the course and there must be complete relief from all interference by the abnormal course condition.


How much of the marked area is considered G.U.R?


  • All ground inside the edge of the defined area, and
  • Any grass, bush, tree or other growing or attached natural object rooted in the defined area, including any part of those objects that extends up above the ground outside the edge of the defined area, but not any part (such as a tree root) that is attached to or below the ground outside the edge of the defined area.

    • What is the reference point for taking a drop?


    The nearest point of complete relief in the general area. If this point forces your stance to be in the area where you are receiving relief from, you can move the reference point up to the point where you are no longer standing in this section.


    How to drop a ball


    Mark the point where you will be dropping (with a tee, pitch tool or other). A ball should be dropped from knee height straight down beside the marker. If the ball rolls from the general area that is marked, then you can drop it again. If it rolls more than twice then you can place the ball in the marked area by hand.
